mscope

Language: English & Spanish
Platform: Desktop
Tools: Figma, Notion, Linear
Service: Design system design

mscope is a SaaS product that helps financial institutions identify high-potential SMEs and facilitate collaborations to accelerate their growth and increase their value.

Goal

Create a modular and scalable Design System that enables the design and development teams to work more efficiently in building an accessible, coherent, and consistent product.

Challenges

· Define and document design foundations clearly and precisely to facilitate collaboration between design and development.

· Design reusable and scalable components following the Atomic design methodology.

· Ensure coherence across the entire interface and teams.

Process

Audit
· Review and identification of inconsistencies (components, patterns, and styles).
· Analysis of detected issues.
Definition of tockens & foundations
· Creation of design tokens.
· Definition of a clear and consistent visual system.
Components design
· Application of Atomic design.
· Development of components and variables.
Handoff
· System documentation (decisions, elements and uses).

1. Audit

I conduced a visual and code audit in collaboration
with development team to identify and assets the impact
of the issue.
Visual inconsistency
Disorganized and inconsistent use of typography, colors, styles, icons.
Component duplication
Repeated and redundant creation of elements with variations in design and code.
Lack of a common library
No centralized resource library. Components developed independently by different teams.
Confused user experience
Disorganized visual structure and inconsistent patterns in navigation and content.

2. Tokens & foundations

The definition of tokens and foundations is approached
from two perspectives:
- Providing users with a pleasant experience by facilitating
data readability and action.
- Creating a solid and scalable system capable of adapting
to new needs in an agile and efficient manner.

3. Components design

Component design is defined following Atomic design methodology. Its structure is intended to be flexible,
robust and reusable, with the ability to efficiently adapt to different needs.Usability testing with internal clients led to improvements based on their feedback.

4. Documentation & Handoff

The goal of the documentation is to ensure efficient collaboration between designers and developers by providing clear usage guidelines and technical specifications that promote consistent use and proper implementation.

Results

Improved product consistency
The interface delivers a unified and coherent experience, enabling users to understand the information, navigation and achieve their goals intuitively and without visual overload.
Increased development efficiency
Documented, modular and reusable components enhance the comunication between design and development, reduce implementation time and allow teams to focus on new goals.
Flexible and scalable growth
Faster integration of new functionalities without compromising quality, making it easier to scale and grow the product.
