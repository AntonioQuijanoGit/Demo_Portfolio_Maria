ivy decarb is a marketplace in the textile machinery sector that conects manufacturers, buyers and financial institutions with the main of facilitating the purchase and sale of machinery that complais with descarbonization regulations and accelerating the process across the value chain.

Goal

Design a product from scratch that ensure an efficient buying and selling process for all stakeholders, providing valuable information and functionalities that support decision-making.

Challenges

· Identifying key needs for each stakeholder and defining valuable functionalities for each enviroment of the product.

· Building a flexible and scalable product that can adapt to new needs and sustainability requeriments.

· Minimizing the learning curve by offering consistent and clear product that provides users with valuable information and guides them toward achieving their goals.

Process

Analysis & Definition
· Needs analysis and solution definition.
· Functional definition of the product and assessment of scope and functional scalability.
Design & Prototyping
· Creation of a design system (foundations, tokens, modular components, and usage documentation).
· High-fidelity prototype design.
Handoff
· Delivery of technical and functional documentation.
‍
Optimize
· Prototype review with stakeholders, identification and implementation of improvements.

2. Interaction design

Information architecture is defined based on the specific goals of each user, with clear and purposeful hierarchies.
Consistent navigation and interaction patterns are established across all product enviroments to support content findability and task efficiency.

2. Design system & Prototype design

Visual foundations were defined to ensure coherency and consistency across the entire product,alining the decisions with a minimalist identy that reflects descarbonization values.
The visual language was designed to be clear and action-oriented. On top of this foundation, a scalable UI Kit was developed, built with modular and reusable components following atomic design methodology. The system was crafted with practical focus-prioritizing consistent experiences, streamlining design workflows, and facilitating implementation during development.

Alignment between value proposition and experience
The design prioritises goal-driven decision making, helping users quickly understand what they can do, why, and how to obtain value with minimal effort.
Scalability enabled through role-based environments
Role-specific spaces (buyer, seller, financier) allow the product to adapt to particular needs without compromising overall system coherence.
Solid design foundations
Reusable patterns, clear hierarchies and a consistent system accelerate development and ensure long-term product consistency.
