mscope

Language: English & Spanish
Platform: Desktop
Tools: Figma, Notion, Linear
Service: Product design

mscope is a SaaS product that helps financial institutions identify high-potential SMEs and facilitate collaborations to accelerate their growth and increase their value.

Goal

Create a collaborative tool that allows users to analyze, group, track, and share companies through customized lists.

Challenges

· Minimize the learning curve of the new tool.

· Design a tool that facilitates effective team collaboration to increase the value and usage of the SaaS.

· Provide transparency regarding access and the privacity of shared information.

Process

Analysis & Definition
· Review and identification of inconsistencies (components, patterns, and styles).
· Analysis of detected issues.
Design & Prototyping
· Creation of design tokens.
· Definition of a clear and consistent visual system.
Handoff
· Delivery of technical and functional documentation.
‍
Test & Optimize
· Product review with users, identification and implementation of improvements.

1. Analysis & Definition

Based on the needs identified during client interviews,
we defined solutions that would bring utility and value
to the product.

Insight
User need
Actionable solution

“...After analysing companies here, I move everything to Excel to track the ones I’m interested in and compare them.”

• Organisation.

• Design customisable lists to group, edit and prioritise companies without leaving the platform.

• “…Comparing several companies is slow; I need to open multiple tabs and I lose the global view.”

• Clarity.

• Create a parallel comparison module showing the key indicators of each company in a single view.

• “…Here we can’t share anything with the team nor comment on opportunities or progress.”

• Collaboration.

• Enable shared lists and editable comments to centralise analysis and communication inside the product.

• “…If something important changes in a company or in our relationship with them, I never find out.”

• Updates.

• Implement configurable alerts that notify relevant variations in real time.

2. Design & Prototyping

Both the IA and the interactions were defined following the logic implemented in the rest of the product, with the goal of incorporating the functionality in the most intuitive way possible.
The components were designed with a reusability approach, aiming to present information clearly and accessibility, and to guide the user toward action.  

3. Handoff

The product requirements documentation integrated technical specifications, context, interaction flows, and error cases, establishing a solid foundation for implementation and ensuring alignment with the product vision. In Figma, a prototype faithful to the PRD was developed to provide clarity, consistency, and facilitate implementation.

4. Test & Optimize

We presented the product to clients to identify potential frictions and validate that it met their needs. Their feedback revealed opportunities for improvement and helped optimize the experience, increasing satisfaction and perceived value.

Users don’t understand what actions are available after selecting companies.

Display actions automatically at the same visual level to improve clarity and accessibility.

Users get frustrated when they can’t manage a list from the general section.

Add key list management actions directly to the general section for faster access.

Relying solely on text comments for classification is slow and inefficient.

Introduce a column with predefined tags and filtering options to streamline data organization.
